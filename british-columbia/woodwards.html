Asphalt vs. interlocking vs. concrete — which is best for Woodwards?
In Woodwards's climate, asphalt is the most popular choice — it handles freeze-thaw cycles well and is cost-effective. Interlocking offers a premium look. Concrete is very durable but requires expansion joints in cold climates. Your contractor can advise based on your budget and preferences.
How long does a new driveway installation take in Woodwards?
A standard residential driveway in Woodwards takes 1–2 days to complete. Asphalt needs 24–48h to cure before driving on it. Interlocking can take 2–4 days.
When is the best time to pave a driveway in Woodwards?
In Woodwards, the paving season runs from late spring to early fall (May–October). Asphalt should not be installed when temperatures are below 10°C. Spring and summer are the most popular booking times — plan ahead.
How long does an asphalt driveway last in Woodwards?
A well-installed asphalt driveway in Woodwards typically lasts 20–30 years with seal coating every 3–5 years and prompt crack repair. Poor base preparation is the main cause of premature failure.
